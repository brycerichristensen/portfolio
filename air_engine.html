Pipsqueak Engine

The "Pipsqueak" is a small-scale, oscillating-cylinder steam engine designed to run on compressed air. This project involved full-cycle mechanical development, from initial CAD modeling and technical drafting to precision machining in the shop.

CAD Modeling & Design

I started by modeling the entire assembly in SolidWorks. The design features a simple oscillating cylinder mechanism where the cylinder itself pivots to open and close the intake and exhaust ports, eliminating the need for a complex valve train.

Technical Drawing

Detailed engineering drawings were created for each component, including the base, upright, cylinder, and flywheel. I focused on proper tolerancing and GD&T to ensure that the moving parts would maintain a tight seal while still allowing for smooth rotation.

Bill of Materials (BOM)

To prepare for manufacturing, I developed a complete Bill of Materials. This included raw stock selection—primarily brass for the cylinder and aluminum for the base—along with the specific fasteners and springs required for the tensioning mechanism.

Machining and Final Assembly

The final engine was manufactured using manual lathes and mills. Precision was key, particularly when lapping the mating surfaces between the cylinder and the upright to prevent air leaks. The result is a robust, functional air engine that operates at high RPMs.
