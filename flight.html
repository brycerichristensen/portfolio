Flight Configuration

Software Architecture

For the 2026 flight, I developed a comprehensive Finite State Machine (FSM) implemented in C++. This logic controls the propulsion unit, ground station, and fill trailer simultaneously, ensuring safety-critical transitions during the fueling and launch sequences.

Custom PCB Design

To reduce wiring complexity and improve signal integrity, I designed a custom PCB to interface our ESP32 controllers with the engine sensors. This design integrates voltage regulation, thermocouple amplifiers, and robust connectors suited for high-vibration flight environments.

PCU Hardware Integration

The Propulsion Control Unit (PCU) is the "brain" of the flight vehicle. I led the development of the internal electronics sled and the final subassembly housing. This modular design allows for rapid bench-testing and seamless integration into the rocket's airframe.

Full Flight Assembly

This CAD model represents the complete propulsion configuration for the 2026 launch. I collaborated with the mechanical team to ensure all electronic components, actuators, and plumbing lines were optimized for weight distribution and ease of access during pre-flight checks.

Laboratory Testing

Validation begins in the lab. Here, the PCU undergoes rigorous stress tests, ranging from long-duration communication stability checks to sensor calibration sweeps, ensuring the hardware is flight-ready.

Fill Trailer Control System

Ground operations are managed by a custom fill trailer. I designed the electronics system that monitors tank pressures and manages the remote fueling process, communicating wirelessly with the vehicle's flight computer.

Ground Station Telemetry

I developed a custom dashboard to monitor real-time telemetry. This interface provides critical data visualizations for both the rocket and ground support equipment, allowing the controls team to make informed "Go/No-Go" decisions during the launch sequence.

Burst Testing & Safety

Safety is paramount in hybrid propulsion. We conduct destructive burst tests on our propellant tanks to verify factor of safety calculations and ensure our structural designs can withstand extreme flight pressures.

Block 2 Development

The "Block 2" engine represents our next iteration in hybrid performance. I am currently adapting the control architecture to accommodate higher flow rates and more complex valve timing for this high-thrust configuration.
