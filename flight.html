Flight Configuration

The flight configuration represents the integration of our control systems into the actual vehicle airframe. Moving from a lab setting to the rocket requires robust hardware, safety-critical software, and wireless ground-to-air communication. This system is designed to manage the launch sequence and maintain real-time telemetry from ignition through flight.

Software Architecture

I developed a comprehensive Finite State Machine (FSM) implemented in C++. This logic manages safety-critical transitions during fueling and launch, coordinating the propulsion unit, ground station, and fill trailer simultaneously.

Custom PCB Design

I designed a custom PCB to interface the ESP32 flight computer with our sensor suite. This reduced potential failure points by eliminating bulk wiring and integrated voltage regulation and signal conditioning for thermocouples and pressure transducers.

Propulsion Control Unit (PCU)

The PCU is the core of the flight electronics. I led the development of the internal sled (shown top right) and the integrated subassembly (bottom right). This modular design allows the entire electronics package to slide into the rocket's avionics bay for easy maintenance and testing.

Integrated Assembly

This CAD model shows the flight-ready propulsion configuration. I worked closely with the mechanical team to ensure the PCU and plumbing were optimized for center-of-gravity requirements and flight vibrations.

Lab Validation

Hardware validation is performed in the lab before integration. Here, we run full launch simulations to check state transition timing and wireless communication reliability under various power loads.

Fill Trailer Control

The fill trailer acts as our ground support equipment. I designed the electronics system that manages remote fueling, ensuring our team remains at a safe distance while monitoring tank pressures wirelessly.

Ground Station UI

I developed a custom ground station interface to monitor real-time telemetry. This provides the team with live pressure readings, valve states, and system health checks during the countdown.

Burst Testing

To ensure structural integrity, we perform destructive burst tests on our propellant tanks. My team monitors these tests to validate factor-of-safety calculations and structural FEA models.

Block 2 Engine

This is the flight-ready Block 2 engine. My controls work on this configuration focused on optimizing flow-rate precision and valve timing for the hybrid combustion cycle.
