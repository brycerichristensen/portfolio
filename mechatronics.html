Mechatronics Rover

Working with a group of four, I designed an autonomous line-following robot for a mechatronics class which was capable of completing a variety of tasks.

Course Navigation Challenge

The course objectives included completing five tasks within a 60-second time limit. The autonomous rover had to exit the lander, follow the white line, collect the sample, deposit the sample, navigate the canyon, and re-enter the lander, beaming a signal to the satellite above.

Electronic System Design

I was primarily responsible for the electronics on my team. As shown in the wiring diagram, I mapped each sensor to the appropriate pin on a PIC24F microcontroller and ensured the correct power was distributed to each node.

Custom PCB Design

Once the wiring diagram was finalized, I converted it into a PCB design, routing each wire to appropriate locations around the board. I made sure that the design was manufacturable and then milled it at the EcEn shop on campus.

PCB Assembly

Once the board was manufactured, I soldered the correct components in each location, testing the continuity of each solder joint. I then wired up the sensors and outputs to test the functionality of the custom PCB.

Sample Collection System

In order to reliably collect and deposit the sample (ping-pong ball), we designed a funnel system that stored the ball between collection and deposition. An optical detector sensor was used to detect whether the ball was black or white, and the robot's finite state machine was programmed to respond accordingly. Once the rover was in position, a servomotor actuated an arm, releasing the sample.

Line Following

For line following, we incorporated five reflectance sensors which detected whether they were on a black or white surface. The microcontroller incorporated a PID control system to provide the feedback necessary for high-speed line following.

Final Prototype

This is the fully assembled autonomous rover. The final design integrated mechanical, electrical, and software elements to produce a robust autonomous system ready for competition.
