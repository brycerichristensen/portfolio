Custom PCB Design

LOREM IPSUM: To move from a breadboard prototype to a flight-ready system, I designed a custom PCB. This layout was created to minimize the overall footprint of the electronics while ensuring robust connections for the ESP32 and peripheral components. This step was crucial for weight reduction and reliability during high-speed maneuvers.

Fabrication & Assembly

LOREM IPSUM: Once the boards were manufactured, I performed the surface-mount and through-hole soldering. This physical implementation allowed for a much cleaner assembly compared to traditional wiring, significantly reducing the risk of loose connections during the mechanical vibration of the course run.

Mechanical Payload System

LOREM IPSUM: The rover featured a specialized mechanism for handling and deploying ball payloads. This required precise mechanical tolerances and integration with the control software to ensure the balls were released at the correct triggers during the course navigation.

Sensor Integration

LOREM IPSUM: Line following and boundary detection were achieved using QRD reflectance sensors. I developed algorithms to calibrate these sensors against varying floor conditions, allowing the rover to maintain high speeds while staying centered on the track.
