Mechatronics Rover

Placeholder: This project involved the design, fabrication, and programming of an autonomous rover capable of navigating a complex obstacle course. Using a combination of sensor fusion, custom PCB design, and mechanical actuation, the rover was optimized for speed and reliability in a competitive environment.

Course Navigation Challenge

LOREM IPSUM: This image shows the complex layout the rover had to navigate autonomously. The course required a mix of high-speed straights, sharp turns, and precise positioning to interact with course elements. My control logic was designed to handle these transitions seamlessly.

System Architecture

LOREM IPSUM: The electrical system was organized to ensure high signal integrity between the ESP32 and the sensors. By mapping out the wiring diagram beforehand, I was able to identify potential EMI issues and plan for the custom PCB implementation.

Custom PCB Design

LOREM IPSUM: The PCB was designed to act as the "brain" of the rover. It integrated motor drivers, power regulation, and headers for the sensor arrays, allowing for a compact and vibration-resistant electronics package.

Fabrication & Assembly

LOREM IPSUM: Soldering the final board required precision to ensure reliable connections for the micro-components. This custom board significantly reduced the weight of the rover compared to a standard breadboard setup.

Mechanical Payload System

LOREM IPSUM: A dedicated mechanical system was engineered to handle the ball payload. This involved mechanical design in SolidWorks to ensure the gravity-fed system worked reliably during movement.

Sensor Integration

LOREM IPSUM: The QRD reflectance sensors provided the feedback necessary for high-speed line following. These were mounted low to the ground to maximize sensitivity and response time.

The Final Prototype

LOREM IPSUM: This is the fully assembled Mechatronics Rover. The final design represents an integration of mechanical engineering, electronics, and software, resulting in a robust autonomous system ready for competition.
