Phoenix Payload
IREC 2026

As a Payload Engineer for BYU’s team, I competed in the 2026 SDL Payload Challenge at the Intercollegiate Rocket Engineering Competition (IREC). Our project, Phoenix, was designed to address the limitations of traditional chemical batteries in deep space, specifically focusing on thermal degradation and dormancy failure.

The Phoenix payload serves as a proof-of-concept mechanical battery utilizing Negator-spring Rotary Generators (NRG). By storing energy through mechanical deflection rather than chemical reactions, the system delivers constant, stable power regardless of thermal environment.

System Architecture

The core of the payload is an array of 12 Negator-spring Rotary Generators (NRGs) featuring 192 interlocking strips of metal wound into springs. We utilized a planetary gear arrangement to create a volume-efficient hollow central sun, which provided the necessary space for the motor.

The structural frame consisted of custom CNC-machined aluminum end caps and angle brackets, which secured the internal components during the extreme vibration profiles of supersonic flight.

Bench Testing & Validation

Before flight, we conducted an extensive testing campaign. Using an MTS apparatus, we characterized the torque-displacement curves to verify the theoretical energy capacity of 211 J. This data allowed us to optimize the ratchet locking mechanism, where a mechanical pawl held the spring energy until the solenoid triggered the release.

Flight Performance & Results

Phoenix was successfully flight-validated aboard the Snowbird rocket at IREC. The structural frame and integrated NRG module remained fully intact with zero damage after enduring a Mach 1.8 supersonic flight to an apogee of 23,000 feet.

Post-flight data confirms the system successfully regenerated energy profiles matching our lab characterization. The system demonstrates significant potential as a rugged, high-cycle mechanical energy reserve for long-duration missions, such as satellite backup or lunar rover power kickstarting.
