Other Rocketry Projects

Beyond specialized propulsion and controls research, I have designed, manufactured, and launched multiple high-power rockets. These projects provided hands-on experience with composite layups, avionics integration, and flight simulation software.

Bomb Pop

Bomb Pop is a 9-foot-tall, dual-deploy rocket that competed in BYU’s Advanced Launch Competition. Powered by an L1000 motor, it achieved an apogee of 8,055 feet—landing just 55 feet from the target. My team and I utilized OpenRocket simulations to ensure high accuracy in our models. The rocket was successfully recovered and earned first place in the competition.

Legatus (Level 2)

Legatus is my Tripoli Level 2 certification rocket. I fabricated the fins using carbon fiber plate layups and rolled the body tubes on a custom mandrel. The rocket features a custom 3D-printed avionics bay housing a GPS telemetry system and a hand-soldered flight computer, providing experience with Surface-Mount Technology (SMT).

The avionics bay (shown right) was designed to be modular, allowing for easy access to the flight computer and dual-deployment charges. The system was simulated for a J270 motor to reach an apogee of 5,000 feet.

Praetor (Level 1)

Praetor served as my Level 1 certification rocket, launching to 1,000 feet in Spring 2025. It featured my first custom avionics integration, capturing live telemetry and onboard video for post-flight analysis.

1776

1776 competed in the BYU student launch competition, reaching nearly 1,700 feet. The project focused on stability and recovery reliability, ultimately earning our team 3rd place out of more than 25 competing teams.
