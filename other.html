Other Rocketry Projects

Bomb Pop

Bomb Pop is a 9-foot-tall, dual-deploy rocket that competed in BYU’s Advanced Launch Competition. Powered by an L1000 motor, it achieved an apogee of 8,055 feet— just 55 feet from the target. My team and I utilized OpenRocket simulations to ensure high accuracy in our models. We developed a live telemtry and onboard video system for the payload, which helped us locate the rocket's position after launch. Bomb Pop was successfully recovered and earned first place in the competition.

Legatus (Level 2)

Legatus is my Tripoli Level 2 certification rocket. I fabricated the fins using carbon fiber plate layups and rolled the carbon fiber body tubes on an aluminum mandrel. It reached an apogee of 4000 feet and was sucessfully recovered with no damage.

Legatus features a custom 3D-printed avionics bay, housing the flight computer, recovery electronics, and an onboard camera, shown to the right. The flight computer was hand-soldered, which gave me ample experience using both through-hole and surface-mount technology (SMT).
