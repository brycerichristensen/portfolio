Other Rocketry Projects

I have designed, manufactured, and launched multiple high-power rockets, which have given me experience with simulation software, avionics, composites, and more. Check them out below!

Bomb Pop

Bomb Pop is a 9-foot-tall, dual-deploy rocket that competed in BYU’s Advanced Launch Competition. Powered by an L1000 motor, it achieved an apogee of 8,055 feet—landing just 55 feet from the target. My team and I carefully simulated the flight to ensure our models were accurate, capturing live telemetry and onboard video throughout. Bomb Pop was successfully recovered without damage, earning first place in the competition. The videos below show footage from the launch.

Legatus

Legatus is my Tripoli Level 2 certification rocket, scheduled to fly in October 2025. Powered by a J270 motor, it is simulated to reach an apogee just over 5,000 feet. I fabricated the fins using carbon fiber plate layups and rolled the carbon fiber body tubes on a mandrel. Additionally, I designed and 3D-printed a custom avionics bay to house the onboard camera, GPS telemetry system, and dual-deploy mechanism. The flight computer was hand-soldered, giving me hands-on experience with surface-mount technology (SMT).

Praetor

Praetor was my Tripoli Level 1 certification rocket, which launched to around 1,000 feet in spring 2025. It also contained a custom-designed avionics bay with onboard video and live telemetry capabilities. See the video below for the in-flight analysis.

1776

1776 competed in BYU’s student launch competition last year, reaching nearly 1,700 feet and earning my team 3rd place out of more than 25 teams.
