Other Rocketry Projects

I have designed, manufactured, and launched multiple high-power rockets, which have given me experience with simulation software, avionics, composites, and more. Check them out below!

Bomb Pop

Bomb Pop was my team’s advanced launch competition rocket, powered by an L1000 motor. It reached an apogee of 8,055 feet AGL—only 55 feet from the target altitude. Featuring dual-deploy recovery, live telemetry, and an onboard camera, the project earned 1st place in the competition.

Legatus

Legatus was my Tripoli Level 2 certification rocket, flown on a J270 motor. I fabricated it using carbon fiber and designed a custom avionics bay. The rocket included dual-deploy recovery and an onboard camera.

Custom hand-soldered avionics bay (including SMT)

Praetor

Praetor was my Level 1 certification rocket, flown on an H135 motor. It featured dual-deploy capability, live telemetry integration, and an onboard camera system.

1776

1776 was a student launch competition rocket powered by a G74 motor. The project placed 3rd out of about 25 rocket teams.
