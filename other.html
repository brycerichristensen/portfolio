Other Rocketry Projects

These projects showcase additional rocketry designs and experiments, highlighting hands-on experience with mechanical systems, control electronics, and flight testing. Scroll down to explore each sub-project.

Bomb Pop

For my advanced launch competition rocket, Bomb Pop, I worked with an L1000 motor and designed it with dual-deploy recovery and live telemetry. I integrated an onboard camera and carefully refined the flight systems to reach an apogee of 8,055 feet AGL — only 55 feet from our target. This project earned first place in the competition, reflecting both precise engineering and execution.

I documented the launch with a vertical video, showing the rocket’s ascent and dual-deploy recovery in real time.

Legatus

Legatus is my Tripoli L2 certification rocket, powered by a J270 motor and fabricated using carbon fiber. I designed a custom avionics bay and integrated an onboard camera. I hand-soldered the flight computer, including SMT components, and ensured dual-deploy recovery for safe landings. This project challenged me to combine advanced fabrication techniques with precise electronics integration.

The second image illustrates the final Legatus assembly, highlighting the finished structure and electronics setup.

Praetor

Praetor is my Tripoli L1 certification rocket, powered by an H135 motor. I equipped it with live telemetry and an onboard camera for data capture during flights. This project emphasized compact design and robust electronics integration.

A horizontal video demonstrates the rocket’s flight, showcasing the telemetry and camera functionality in action.

1776

1776 is a student launch competition rocket, powered by a G74 motor. I worked on the design and assembly, ultimately earning 3rd place out of approximately 25 teams. This project helped me hone rapid prototyping skills and practical rocketry experience.
