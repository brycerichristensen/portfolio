Vertical-Landing Leg Deployment

This project focused on designing and testing a servo-actuated vertical-landing leg deployment system, aimed at enabling precise and reliable rocket landings. This prototype for a vertical-landing model rocket won first place in an introductory electrical engineering competition.

For the initial design, I used SolidWorks to model the landing leg assembly, carefully applying assembly constraints to ensure smooth actuation. I wrote Arduino code to read a sonar range sensor and control three servomotors via PWM, and I designed the electronic circuits to connect power, ground, and signal lines for each component.

I 3D printed and assembled the prototype, paying close attention to tolerances for accurate fit. I integrated the electronics and verified that the system actuated the landing legs reliably when within a set proximity to the ground.

The final setup demonstrates precise, repeatable landing leg deployment, showcasing the combination of mechanical design, electronics, and programming in a single integrated system. This prototype earned first place out of 150 students in an electrical engineering design competition.
