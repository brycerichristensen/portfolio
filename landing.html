Vertical Landing Leg Deployment

This project focuses on designing and testing a servo-actuated vertical-landing leg deployment system, aimed at enabling precise and reliable rocket landings. This prototype for a vertical-landing model rocket won first place in an introductory electrical engineering competition.

I designed the initial landing leg system in SolidWorks, using assembly constraints to ensure precise and reliable actuation. I programmed an Arduino to interpret data from a sonar range sensor and generate PWM signals for three servomotors. Additionally, I designed and wired the electronic circuits to provide power, ground, and signal connections for each component.

I 3D-printed and assembled the prototype, carefully ensuring that all components fit with tight tolerances. I integrated the electronics and conducted functional testing, successfully actuating the landing gear when the rocket reached a preset proximity to the ground. This project earned 1st place among 150 students in an electrical engineering design competition.
