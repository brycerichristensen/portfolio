Static Configuration

The static fire stand is our primary testing environment for engine characterization. By anchoring the propulsion system, we can safely measure thrust, pressure transients, and thermal data. These tests allow us to iterate on our control logic and hardware reliability before any system is cleared for flight integration.

System Timing & Reliability

I modified the LabVIEW control logic to correct critical timing issues between ignition and run-valve actuation. Additionally, I expanded the system's sensing capability from three to five pressure transducers and replaced bulky ignition switches with high-power MOSFETs, making the control box more compact and reliable.

Noise Mitigation & DAQ Refinement

The previous DAQ system suffered from significant sensor noise. I led the effort to resolder pressure transducer protoboards, replace screw terminals with quick-disconnect soldered outlets, and upgrade the voltage regulation system. The stacked images to the right show the "Before" and "After" state of the DAQ wiring, which stabilized our signal floor for higher-fidelity data.

Data Analysis

The graph on the right shows pressure transducer data from a recent hot-fire. PT1 tracks the oxidizer tank pressure drop over the 10-second test. These high-quality results allowed us to calculate combustion efficiency and refine our engine parameters for the next iteration.
